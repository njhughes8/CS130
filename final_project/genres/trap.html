Trap

Trap as a genre is a combination of influences from a wide variety of different genres that may not originally seem to mesh well together. Production elements inspired by another genre called Trap (this being the hip-hop offshoot that began in the South) combined with beat and design elements hailing mostly from dubstep created the Trap that EDM knows and loves today. Trap tends to have a darker melodic tone to it, coupled with more aggressive sounding beats that use low and hard 808s and a whole lot of snare drum action. Though the genre of trap in the EDM world and the rap world are different, the rap influence tends to blend quite well with the electronic elements and it has become very common to combine the two. Artists that have taken off with the trap genre include RL Grime, Dillon Francis, and Marshmello.

Trap Highlight

Flosstradamus has become quite the denizen of trap in recent years, and their track “2MUCH” featuring 24hrs is a perfect example of this. What this track does particularly well as a genre showcase is its use of the aforementioned influences. The dubstep influence is clear - a grimy and gritty drop section on top of a slower beat structure. The rap blend works nicely here as well, as 24hrs’ vocals show just how well the two styles blend together. The hip-hop beat rife with plentiful hi-hats and heavy 808 work brings everything together to create an all-around banging track. The song carries a very powerful energy throughout, one that encourages moshing around and going crazy. This song is a great one for festival listening and college party goers alike. Have fun raging!
