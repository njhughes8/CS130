Dubstep

Enter headbanging. This genre is not for the faint of heart. One of EDM’s heaviest offspring, Dubstep encapsulates what it means for a song to “drop.” I daresay that this is what most non-EDM listeners believe all EDM tends to sound like, and that is not for no reason. It is certainly one of the more standout genres under the EDM umbrella, constituted by hard-hitting and gritty basslines, heavy lead sounds, and a slower rhythm in more of a 2-step fashion. This is the go-to genre for those looking to rage the night away at a festival. Dubstep serves as the foundation for a lot of modern EDM genres, a rather aggressive cornerstone if I do say so myself. Some popular Dubstep producers include Skrillex, Excision, and Teminite.

Dubstep Highlight

Somewhat of a departure from what many associate with dubstep, “New Amsterdam” by Fox Stevenson begins with a lovely vocal performance from Fox himself. But don’t be fooled - this song shows off everything that makes dubstep great. Heavy hitting lead sounds, a driving bassline, and thick kick drums create a drop sequence that is hard not to bop your head back and forth to. This song is also a good example of the 2-step beat structure in comparison to, say, a house track. Kick drums are heavy, but only hit on the 1 and 3 beats of the 4 bar measure. “New Amsterdam” carries a certain energy with it, and that massive energy is exactly what brings people back to Dubstep.
