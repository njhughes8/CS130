Glitch Hop

Glitch Hop is a very fun genre to talk about, mainly because it is known for being quite funky. A fun blend of EDM, beats with inspiration from hip-hop, and sounds reminiscent of video games gave Glitch Hop its start, but over time the genre has moved away from the hip-hop influence and steadily towards trap and dubstep inspiration. When you think “glitch,” think of track skipping, distorted audio, vocal chops, cutting, or anything that would make you think of a track as “glitchy.” This genre is also sometimes referred to as simply 110 BPM because that’s where the genre generally resides. 110 BPM is a very recognizable tempo as well, especially when compared to faster house tempos or even faster Hardcore tempos. A couple of notable Glitch Hop creators include Teminite, Rob Gasser, and TheFatRat.
