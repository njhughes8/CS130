Melodic Dubstep

Take everything I said about Dubstep being a foundational genre, and add a bit of euphoria. That truly is the best word to describe Melodic Dubstep as a genre. Clearly not a far deviation from Dubstep itself, MelDub uses the same beat structures and many of the same instrumental elements but with production that brings euphoric emotion to the forefront of the listeners’ minds. While technically a subgenre of Dubstep, MelDub’s typical stylistic elements are so wildly different and honestly closer to a future bass sound that it warrants its own entry on my list. This genre is often very vocal driven and melody focused, as the name would suggest. One might find powerful chords and harmonious sound design when listening to this type of EDM. The genre also provides a lot of room for acoustic instrumentals than a lot of EDM does, perhaps a piano or a guitar intro leading into a hard-hitting melody. A few notable Melodic Dubstep producers include Seven Lions, ILLENIUM, and William Black.

Melodic Dubstep Highlight

MiTiS is one of my current favorite Melodic Dubstep artists out there, and his song “Try” is one of his best to date. Piano chords and acoustic guitar performance brings about a layer of emotion, which is carried on by RØRY’s beautiful vocals and brought to a peak with a truly uplifting bass sound. Hard hitting synths over a 2-step beat sounds like it would be associated with regular ol’ Dubstep, but euphoria wins out in this song due to emotionally charged production throughout the track. Try to sit around and listen to this song when you find yourself mad or unhappy in any way. Difficult to stay that way, right? MiTiS has been absolutely killing the game lately, and this song is a perfect example of why.
