Deep House

Deep House is a genre of house music with homegrown roots. Originating in Chicago, IL in the late 80s, Deep House spins classic house structures with elements of funk and generally has less and/or more muted percussion than other house genres. It also tends to be slower than other house genres (around 110-125 BPM), which creates a more groove-centered structure compared to a faster, make-you-want-to-jump framework. In that regard, the more mellow approach to house music is often accompanied by soulful vocals and more minimalistic production. Not to say that the production is worse in any way, just that it comes off as less in-your-face than other genres. Artists who make a lot of great Deep House include Oliver Heldens, Duke Dumont, and EDX.

Deep House Highlight

See what I mean when I said groovy and more mellow? “Cheque” by Don Diablo manages to remain both punchy and infectious while maintaining a more mellow atmosphere compared to something in the Commercial House genre. A smooth set of vocals and a groovable melody coupled with a laid back yet minimal beat structure make this song a perfect party anthem. To top it all off, a low, reverb-filled lead sound gives the track an aura that is, well, deep. I feel like I ought to be walking into a club listening to this song in order to truly match the vibe it gives off.
