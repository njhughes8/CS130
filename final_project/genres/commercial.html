Commercial House

Commercial House is an aptly named genre of electronic music that encapsulates a variety of different house tracks that have a “commercial” feel to them. Generally centered around 128 BPM, this type of house music represents the intersection between top 40 radio hits and house music, hence “commercial.” As such, many house tracks that people know very well can be tagged with this moniker, like “Levels” by Avicii. Radio-friendly vocals, catchy melodies, the classic 4-on-the-floor beat structure, and hard-hitting yet danceable toplines are the key features of this genre. Once widely referred to as “Progressive House,” that title already had a home with a much different sounding branch of house music. This genre of music is really more of a container for house music that has found its way into mainstream listening, but the similarities between those successes were enough to warrant a new name. Some artists you may have heard of that have produced this type of music include David Guetta, Calvin Harris, and Tiësto.

Commercial House Highlight

I really do love this song. “Ghost in the Machine” by Trivecta isn’t a song many of you will have heard of, but is a perfect example of a radio-friendly tune with top notch production. Fantastic uncredited vocals from RØRY and an infectious melody over a house beat make this a song anyone would have a good time listening to. Boasting a somewhat euphoric feel to its instrumental, this track is perfect for both radio play and festival listening. This truly makes me feel like I’m listening to 2012 Calvin Harris for the first time again, which is impressive considering the more modern production value. It’s simply a shame it didn’t blow up in the mainstream like it deserves to.
