Garage

Garage music originated in the UK in the 90s and is perhaps one of the most recognizable genres to the untrained ear due to its rather unique set of samples and beat structures. Heavy use of percussion, syncopated hi-hats, chopped up vocal samples, and common use of breakbeat structures are what set this genre apart. That said, the percussion samples used in a lot of Garage tracks are widely used by most Garage producers, which makes the genre recognizable if one were to single out the kick and snare samples. Garage music tends to be fairly uptempo, sitting around 130 BPM. It also tends to have a more chill aura about it, foregoing the grittier sounds of heavier EDM for a more relaxing feel and letting complex beat work capture the listener instead. Some popular Garage artists include Drinks On Me, Higgo, and CloudNone.

Garage Highlight

Direct has been making Garage tracks for a long time now, and he has truly mastered his craft. His song “I Know” is a great way to show just what Garage is to new listeners, and is one of my favorites of his to date. There really may not be a more perfect example given what I have described above; vocal chops on top of a breakbeat structure are the driving force behind “I Know,” and the common garage kick and snare samples are utilized to their fullest in creating an interesting beat. The production of this song may seem simplistic compared to some of the other songs I have chosen, but I choose to look at that as a plus rather than a downside. Direct produced a song that does not rely on heavy hitting melodies to create the same emotional response. “Less is more,” some have said, and I believe that this track uses less to be way more impactful than a lot of songs. Great music for studying or lying in the sun.
